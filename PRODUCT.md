# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who care about handcrafted goods — the kind of buyer who wants to know *who* made a thing and *how* before they own it. They arrive curious (drawn by a piece, a photo, or word of mouth) and want two things: to see the work and understand the making, and to buy it. They value provenance, material honesty, and durability over disposability. Context: browsing on phone or laptop, often leisure time, deciding whether this maker is worth trusting with their money.

## Product Purpose

Craftasaurus is the public face and storefront for a maker of handcrafted goods — woodworking, blacksmithing / metalwork, and laser-cut wood pieces (signs, boxes, and the like). The site tells the story of how the work is made and sells the finished pieces (commerce via Snipcart). First deliverable: a landing page that communicates what Craftasaurus is and earns enough trust to move someone toward a purchase. Success = a visitor understands the craft, believes in its quality, and can act on that belief.

## Brand Personality

Warm and handmade-artisan. Earthy, tactile, honest. The voice of a skilled maker who lets the work speak — quiet confidence, not salesmanship; wit, not gimmick. Three words: **honest, warm, made-by-hand.** The Craftasaurus name carries character and a wink, but the goods are for grown-ups: personality with a straight face. Emotional goals — trust, warmth, the tactile pull of real materials, the small delight of a name that doesn't take itself too seriously.

## Anti-references

- **Etsy-cluttered / craft-fair busy.** No sticker soup, no five fonts, no twee hobbyist mess. Restraint is how honest craft reads as premium rather than amateur.
- **Generic SaaS / startup.** No gradient blobs, no hero-metric templates, no cool-blue tech sheen. This sells physical objects, not software.
- **Cold minimalist / sterile.** Not so stripped-back it loses the maker's-hands warmth. Empty white space is not the goal; material and texture are.
- **Childish / cartoonish.** Despite the dino name, this is not a kids' toy site. The mascot/wit is a seasoning, never the meal.

The tight box: characterful but not twee, honest craft but not cluttered, refined but never cold, personality-forward but not childish.

## Design Principles

- **Show the making.** Process is the proof of craft — grain, forge marks, sawdust, the tool in hand. Lead with how, not just what.
- **Material honesty.** Wood, steel, fire, laser. Let real materials and their marks carry the identity instead of decoration invented on top.
- **Warmth without clutter.** Restraint is the strategy, not a subtraction of soul. Every element earns its place; the warmth comes from material and craft, not from piling things on.
- **Character with a straight face.** The name gives permission for wit; the goods demand it be understated. Grown-up first, playful second.
- **Built to sell.** This is a storefront. Every page should build the credibility that turns a curious visitor into a buyer — clarity and trust over spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** (a sensible floor for a commerce site): body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, keyboard-navigable, visible focus states, alt text on all product/process imagery. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with non-motion alternatives for any reveal or scroll effect. *(Proposed default — tell me if you need a stricter standard or have specific user needs.)*
